About

I am a Senior Cyber Threat Analyst with a Master of Science in Management of Information Systems specializing in cybersecurity. My work spans threat intelligence, strategic analysis, and instructor-led enablement that translates emerging technologies into practical insight.

Focus Areas

Where I blend analytical practice and independent research interests to deliver clear, actionable insight.

Threat Intelligence

End-to-end adversary tracking, threat hunting, and translating complex telemetry into concise direction for leaders and operators.

AI Systems Insight

Exploring how machine learning and automation augment analysis, while evaluating risks, model trustworthiness, and responsible adoption.

Complex Systems Thinking

Applying network science and information theory to uncover hidden dependencies and strengthen resilience across socio-technical systems.

Research

My research investigates Artificial Intelligence, Network Science, Complex Systems, and Information Theory as distinct threads that inform how I understand complex digital environments, resilience, and human decision-making.

  • Designing analytical frameworks that fuse signals from large-scale data with human expertise.
  • Modeling cascading effects within complex infrastructures to improve anticipatory defense.
  • Studying how information flows can be measured and protected as AI systems evolve.

Teaching & Outreach

I create learning experiences that demystify advanced security concepts for practitioners, helping teams apply rigorous analysis in the flow of their work.

  • Developing curriculum that connects emerging threats with practical detection and response playbooks.
  • Mentoring analysts on analytical tradecraft, communication, and stakeholder alignment.
  • Sharing research insights with multidisciplinary communities to drive collaborative problem solving.
